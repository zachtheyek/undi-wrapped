## 2) LinkedIn

*Attach `undi-wrapped-demo.gif` (or the screen-recording) + 1–2 screenshots.*

> **What if you could see your own constituency's entire electoral history in 30 seconds — on your phone, for free?**
>
> I built **Undi Wrapped**: a "Spotify Wrapped" for any of Malaysia's 222 parliamentary and 600 state seats. Search your kawasan and swipe through a card stack of its story since 1955 — the party that founded it, every party that's won it, its biggest swing, its closest-ever race, how marginal it is today, its turnout versus the national average, and an animation of how its boundaries have morphed across seven decades of redelineation. You can also put your seat head-to-head against a friend's.
>
> The civic idea is simple: most of us can name the PM but couldn't tell you who has held our *own* seat, or how competitive it actually is. Seventy years of official results already answer that — they're just locked in spreadsheets. Undi Wrapped makes them personal, glanceable, and non-partisan.
>
> **Credit where it's overwhelmingly due:** this is built entirely on the **Malaysian Election Corpus (MECo)** by **Thevesh Thevananthan** — every federal and state election since 1955, painstakingly cleaned and standardised over roughly four years and peer-reviewed in *Nature* Scientific Data. My project only puts a friendly interface on his work. The corpus is open (CC0) at electiondata.my.
>
> A note on method and its limits: because Malaysian seats are renumbered and occasionally renamed across delimitations, a seat's history is threaded by name within its state (true boundary lineage comes from MECo's electoral maps); "biggest swing" is defined as the largest single-election change in the winner's vote share. The assumptions and full source are documented in the repo.
>
> 👉 Try your seat: https://zachtheyek.github.io/undi-wrapped/
> 📚 The data: https://electiondata.my · 💻 Source & method: https://github.com/zachtheyek/undi-wrapped
